The industry's transparency problem

Sports betting content runs on unverifiable claims. Records quoted with no ledger behind them. Winning slips posted while losing slips vanish. Cold streaks scrubbed with a rebrand. None of this is illegal, and almost all of it is misleading, because the audience only ever sees the wins.

The test is simple: can you see every graded pick, including the losses? If the answer is no, you are looking at marketing. If the answer is yes, you are looking at a record. TrustMyRecord exists to make the second kind the standard.

Transparency FAQ

What makes betting results transparent?

The full pick history is public: every pick with odds, stake, submission time, and graded outcome, including losses. Anything less is selective reporting.

Why do most handicappers avoid this?

Because selective reporting sells. Showing only wins and quoting uncheckable records is easier than keeping a public ledger.

How does TrustMyRecord enforce it?

Picks lock before games, grade automatically from final scores, and stay on a public ledger that cannot be edited after settlement.

Can I audit a profile myself?

Yes. Open any public profile and read the graded history directly: dates, odds, stakes, results, units, and ROI.
