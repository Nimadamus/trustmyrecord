What makes a pick-tracking community trustworthy?

Transparency is not a vibe. It is a set of mechanics that either exist or they do not.

Picks lock before start time

Each pick is timestamped and locked before the game begins. Nothing can be added or changed once the action starts.

Results are auto-graded

Outcomes come from the final score, graded automatically by the same engine for everyone, not from self-reported scorekeeping.

Records are public

Win or lose, every graded pick stays on a public profile. Losses cannot be deleted to flatter a record.

Units and ROI are visible

Net units, ROI, and win percentage are computed across the full history, so one hot streak cannot carry the math.

Profiles build over time

A record is not a single screenshot. It grows pick by pick into a dated, checkable history tied to one account.

Leaderboards use real data

Rankings are built on verified graded performance, not on how loud or popular an account is.

Why screenshots are not enough

A screenshot feels like proof, but it proves almost nothing. It can be edited in seconds, cropped to hide context, or posted after the result is already known. Losing tickets are simply never shown, so what looks like a 70 percent winner is often a carefully chosen highlight reel. The two things that actually matter are missing: a timestamp before the game and a public history that includes the losses.
