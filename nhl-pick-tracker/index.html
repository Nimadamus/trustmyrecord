Why a basic win-loss record fails in hockey

The NHL punishes simple records harder than any sport. So many games are decided by a single goal that a handicapper can run 12-4 on pure variance, then regress hard the next month, and a screenshot of the hot stretch tells you nothing about which is real. Empty-net goals turn puck-line winners into losers in the final minute. Locked, auto-graded picks cut through it: every one-goal loss, every total that pushed through an overtime marker, every dog that got the backup and folded stays on the page next to the wins, graded the same way every time. Over a long season, only the full record separates edge from noise.
