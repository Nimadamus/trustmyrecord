What separates a real MLB capper from a hot weekend

The daily volume that makes baseball great is also what hides bad bettors. Anyone can fire fifteen moneylines on a Saturday and screenshot the winners. A top MLB handicapper is defined by the opposite habit: a disciplined, repeatable approach that stays green across slumps, bullpen meltdowns and the dog days of August. Because every pick here is locked before first pitch and graded automatically, the ranking surfaces the cappers whose edge survived a long season, not the ones who caught a lucky three-day run.

The metrics that actually matter over 162 games

Sort by net units to see who produced the most profit, and by ROI to see who was most efficient with what they risked. Then weigh it against sample size: in baseball, +6 units over 400 graded picks is a far stronger signal than the same figure over 30, because the season is so long that small samples are almost pure noise. Treat a glossy win percentage on heavy favorites with suspicion, since laying -180 all year can win often and still bleed units.

Frequently asked questions

How are MLB handicappers ranked?
By their verified, auto-graded record across the season. Net units and ROI drive the order, and featured placement needs at least 20 graded picks so a hot weekend over a short baseball stretch cannot top a board built over months of daily games.
Why does sample size matter so much for an MLB capper?
Baseball plays about 2,430 games a year, so variance over a week or two is enormous. A real MLB edge only shows up over hundreds of graded picks, which is why the ranking weights a long, locked record over any recent streak.
