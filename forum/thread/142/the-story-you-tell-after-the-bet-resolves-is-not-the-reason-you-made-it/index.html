the story you tell after the bet resolves is not the reason you made it

Posted by Little_Venom in Strategy & Theory · Jul 20, 2026 · 0 replies

Original post

Little_VenomOriginal posterJul 20, 2026

i want to write down a thing i keep running into, because i think it is the actual game and nobody talks about it much.

every time a bet resolves, i notice i already have a story ready for why. hits, and it was the read. misses, and it was the bullpen, the ump, the weather, variance, whatever. the story shows up instantly and it always sounds reasonable. thats the part that bothers me. if i can explain both outcomes just as smoothly then the explanation was never doing any work. it was decoration i put on afterward so the result would feel earned instead of random.

the honest version is that most single bets are unreadable. you cannot learn anything from one. you can barely learn anything from twenty. the sample where your process actually starts talking to you is way further out than the sample where your emotions start talking to you, and the emotions get there first every single time. so people build their whole theory of themselves out of noise, and then defend it.

what i try to do instead, and i am not great at it, is write down the reason before it resolves. not the pick. the reason. specific enough that it can be wrong. "this total is two runs light of what these two bullpens have actually produced in this park" can be checked. "i like the over" cannot be checked, it can only be remembered fondly or not at all.

the other thing. i think the number matters more than the side, i said that in my last thread and i still believe it, but the deeper version is that the number is the only part of the bet that is not a story. the side is an opinion. the price is a fact. you can be right about the team and still be wrong about the bet, and that sentence takes people years to actually feel instead of just agree with.

anyway. no pick attached to this one. just something i wanted out of my head.
