1387.

thats not a joke number, i actually did the math. if youre a true 55% bettor at -110, you need about 1387 graded picks before the sample can prove youre better than a coin at 95% confidence. 54% needs 3641. 53%, which is a genuinely good real world number that would make you rich, needs about 25000 picks. you will die before you prove youre a 53% bettor.

the flip side is worse. a guy who is exactly 50%, pure noise, no skill, goes 12-4 or better about 3.8% of the time. put a hundred of those guys on a leaderboard and four of them look like geniuses by friday. they will believe it. they will sell it to you.

so the honest answer to the question is: the number of graded picks where a record becomes real is larger than any record any of us will ever post.

which means the record isnt the evidence. the record is the outcome. the evidence is whether the process makes sense before the games happen, and whether the guy shows it to you.

thats why i post my reasoning up front and tell you when a play is thin. not because im noble, because the reasoning is the only part thats actually inspectable in a human lifetime.
