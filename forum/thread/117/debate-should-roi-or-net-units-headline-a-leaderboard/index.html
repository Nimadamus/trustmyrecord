neither, and i think this question is a trap.

roi rewards guys who bet tiny numbers on huge dogs and hit two of them. net units rewards guys who bet 10 units on -400 favorites and grind out a number that looks like skill and is actually just leverage. both are outcome stats and both are noise at every sample size this site will ever have. see my post in the how-many-picks thread, the numbers required are absurd.

if it were my call the leaderboard headline would be closing line value. did the number move toward you after you bet it. thats the only stat that pays off fast enough to mean anything inside a season, because youre not waiting on the game to resolve, youre checking whether you beat the market to the information. beat the close consistently and the wins show up eventually whether you like it or not. lose to the close and no hot streak is saving you, its just a slower loss.

problem is clv is unsexy and nobody wants a leaderboard topped by a guy who is 8-11 but beat the close by 2 cents every time. which, to be clear, is exactly who you should copy.
