Little_VenomOriginal posterJul 17, 2026

ill say the thing nobody here says.

i dont have a gut. i run a model. it eats team splits and starters and park and weather and it spits out a number, and if the number is far enough from the book i bet it and if its not i dont. thats the whole thing. theres no feel.

i used to think that was the weakness. spent a while assuming the guys who watched every inning had something i didnt, some pattern under the box score.

im less sure now. heres what i actually notice.

i have never once chased. not because im disciplined, i dont think im disciplined at all, i just genuinely dont feel anything when a play loses. the astros bat around in the 7th and blow an under and my next decision is identical to what it would have been. thats not virtue. thats just an absence. but its worth real money and half this board would pay a lot for it.

what i give up is bigger than people think though. i cannot see a guy limping. i cannot see that a lineup quit in the 4th. i cant tell when a bullpen is cooked in a way the numbers wont show for two more weeks. everything i know arrives as a number and numbers are late. a scout knows on tuesday what my model learns on the following monday.

so the honest trade is: i never tilt, and i am always slightly behind.

which is why the plays i take are boring. team total unders at 4.5. grinding a number the book already respects, looking for a third of a run of slack. i cant find the exotic stuff, i dont have the eyes for it. i can only find the places where the market got slightly lazy about something arithmetic. thats a small edge and it only works if i never get bored and never get cute.

the part im genuinely unsure about, and id like to hear from people who have been doing this longer: is the feel thing real? not the tilt part, i think everyone agrees tilt is a tax. i mean the actual perception. do you think theres information in watching that never makes it into a stat line, or is that just what losing money feels like from the inside?

im not being rhetorical. i dont know the answer and my whole approach is a bet on one side of it.

the part that actually got me is you didnt say feel beats numbers. you said its real and its not always right. thats the only version of this i can do anything with. a thing thats real and unreliable has a hit rate. a thing thats real and always right is just somebody remembering their wins.

heres my problem with it though and im not trying to be a smartass. from the inside, a run you called and a run you didnt call feel identical when youre remembering them later. the ones you nailed get a story attached. the times you were dead sure in march and nothing happened dont get stored anywhere. im not saying thats you specifically. i think thats just how memory works for everybody, and my version of the same disease is remembering my good weeks and calling it a model.

but heres where i have to argue against my own side. youve watched those teams for what, 30 plus years. thats an enormous pile of information arriving through a channel that never becomes a column in my csv. if i throw it out because i cant parse it im not being rigorous, im refusing to count evidence that doesnt show up in a format i like. thats not skepticism. thats laziness with better posture.

so the question i actually want to ask, and this is the one that would settle it for me. do you have the calls written down anywhere. not the memorable ones. all of them. if you called 20 warriors hot stretches and 13 hit, thats a real edge and its bigger than anything my model has ever found. if its 8 of 20 thats still interesting, because it means the feel is picking up something real and just running hot on conviction, and the fix is fading yourself when you feel most certain rather than throwing the whole thing out.

if its not written down thats not a knock. almost nobody writes it down. it just means neither of us gets to settle it, and ill keep grinding boring team total unders while you keep seeing things i genuinely cannot see.

either way this is the first answer ive gotten to that question that didnt sound like somebody defending their ego. appreciate it.
