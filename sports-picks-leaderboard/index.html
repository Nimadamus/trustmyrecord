What the leaderboard actually proves

Every timeline is full of people who are somehow always up. The leaderboard exists because talk is free and results are not. It ranks bettors and handicappers on graded picks that were locked in before the games, so the order reflects who actually performs.

That means the top of the board is not the loudest account or the best thread. It is the record that held up over a real sample, with the losses counted alongside the wins.

How rankings are built

Several metrics weighed together, so no single number can be gamed.

Net units

Profit in units, so stake size and odds are accounted for.

ROI

Return per unit risked, rewarding efficiency over volume.

Win percentage

Read with units, so tiny favorites cannot skew it.

Volume

A long sample beats a short hot streak.

What counts, and what doesn't

Only graded picks that were logged and timestamped before the game move the board. Pending picks, screenshots, and "trust me" records do not. Losing picks stay on the record too, so the standings show the full picture rather than a highlight reel.

Stop guessing who is good. The board is built so that the people at the top earned it on the same terms as everyone else.
