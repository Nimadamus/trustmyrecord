Why net units is the right way to score an MLB contest

Baseball is a moneyline sport, and that makes raw win-loss records misleading. An entrant who hammers minus-200 favorites can post a shiny win percentage while losing units, and a disciplined underdog player can win fewer games and finish well ahead. Ranking by net units fixes that: every pick is settled at its submitted odds, so the board rewards profitable handicapping rather than safe-looking records.

It is the same standard used across TrustMyRecord, from the picks leaderboard to every public profile. If a scoring system would not survive on a real bankroll, it does not belong in a contest.

A contest run on verification, not screenshots

Plenty of MLB contests run on the honor system. This one runs on the TrustMyRecord engine: picks timestamped at submission, sealed until first pitch, graded automatically from final scores, and displayed on a public board. The standings are the proof. When the contest ends, the winners have a graded, inspectable record of how they got there.
