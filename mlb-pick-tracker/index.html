Why baseball records are the easiest to fake

No sport gives a tout more cover than MLB. Frequently asked questions

What happens to my pick if the probable pitcher is scratched?
Your locked pick keeps the price and listing you committed to, with its timestamp. Listed-pitcher conditions are graded by the rules in effect when you logged it, so a late scratch cannot be used to quietly improve or void a record after the fact.
Which MLB markets can I track?
Moneylines, run lines, game totals, team totals, first 5 innings (F5) lines, NRFI and YRFI first-inning totals, and player props such as strikeouts, hits, total bases, RBIs and home runs. Each locks before first pitch and auto-grades on the official result.
Why is verification more important in baseball than other sports?
With around 2,430 regular-season games plus the playoffs, a tout can play dozens of moneylines a day and simply post the winners. The huge daily volume is exactly what makes selective posting so easy, and why a locked, fully graded record is the only way to trust a baseball number.
How are first 5 innings and NRFI picks graded?
F5 picks settle on the score after five complete innings, and NRFI/YRFI settle on whether a run scores in the first inning, independent of the final score. Both lock before first pitch and grade automatically.
