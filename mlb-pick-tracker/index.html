What the MLB pick tracker does

When you log a MLB pick, TrustMyRecord stores the exact selection and odds and locks a timestamp before the game starts. That locked timestamp is the heart of verification: it proves you committed to the pick at a specific number, so no result can be rewritten after the fact. Once the game goes final, the auto-grader reads the official result and settles your pick automatically, then updates your record, net units, ROI, average odds, win rate, and current streak.

You can track all the MLB markets you actually bet, including moneylines, run lines, totals, team totals, first-five innings, and player props (hits, total bases, strikeouts, earned runs). Every graded pick rolls into the same public record, so your long-term performance is visible and auditable.

Why verified MLB records matter

Baseball is a long-season, high-volume sport, which makes it ideal for honest record tracking: 162 games per team means a real sample builds quickly, and a handful of lucky nights cannot hide a losing approach over time. Anyone can post a screenshot of a winner. A verified record is different: it includes the losers too, locked before the result was known, graded the same way every time. That is the only honest way to judge whether a MLB handicapper is actually good.

Units, ROI, average odds, and sample size

TrustMyRecord scores MLB performance the way sharp bettors actually think about it. Units measure profit independent of how much money you bet. ROI shows return per unit risked, which exposes whether someone profits or just wins low-value favorites. Average odds reveal the kind of prices a handicapper plays, and sample size tells you how much to trust the number. A +8 unit record over 300 picks means far more than a hot week.
