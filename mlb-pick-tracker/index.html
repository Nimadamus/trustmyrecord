Every MLB bet type, from the moneyline to NRFI

Baseball gives you more ways to be right or wrong than any other sport on the board. The tracker grades each one off the official box score:

Moneyline

The team to win outright. Baseball bakes no points into the result, so MLB moneylines stretch from heavy -210 aces down to live +150 underdogs. Your locked average odds expose whether you are paying up for favorites or getting paid on dogs.

Run line (-1.5 / +1.5)

Baseball's fixed 1.5-run spread. Laying -1.5 with a favorite pays a plus price but needs a two-run win; taking +1.5 buys the one-run insurance this sport is famous for. Both settle on the final margin.

Totals and team totals

Over or under on combined runs, or on a single club's runs. Wind, park factors and bullpen fatigue swing these a full run, which is exactly why the number you locked is frozen before the lineups and the forecast move it.

First 5 innings (F5)

The score after five complete innings. F5 isolates the starting pitchers and cuts out the bullpen entirely, and the tracker grades it on the five-inning line no matter what happens late.

NRFI / YRFI

No Run First Inning or Yes Run First Inning, baseball's most popular micro-market. It settles on the first inning alone, independent of the final score, and locks before first pitch like everything else.

The MLB prop board: pitchers and hitters, graded both ways

Props are where baseball bettors actually live, and TrustMyRecord grades both halves of the matchup. On the mound: pitcher strikeouts, outs recorded, hits allowed, earned runs and walks issued. What happens to my pick if the probable pitcher is scratched?
Your locked pick keeps the price and listing you committed to, with its timestamp. Listed-pitcher conditions are graded by the rules in effect when you logged it, so a late scratch cannot be used to quietly improve or void a record after the fact.
Which MLB markets can I track?
Moneylines, run lines, game totals, team totals, first 5 innings (F5) lines, NRFI and YRFI first-inning totals, and player props such as strikeouts, hits, total bases, RBIs and home runs. Each locks before first pitch and auto-grades on the official result.
Why is verification more important in baseball than other sports?
With around 2,430 regular-season games plus the playoffs, a tout can play dozens of moneylines a day and simply post the winners. The huge daily volume is exactly what makes selective posting so easy, and why a locked, fully graded record is the only way to trust a baseball number.
How are first 5 innings and NRFI picks graded?
F5 picks settle on the score after five complete innings, and NRFI/YRFI settle on whether a run scores in the first inning, independent of the final score. Both lock before first pitch and grade automatically.
