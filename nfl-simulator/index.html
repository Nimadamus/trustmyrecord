Simulate any NFL matchup

The TrustMyRecord NFL game simulator plays out any matchup thousands of times and returns a projected final score, a calibrated win probability, spread and total analysis, and a full reconciled box score with realistic player statistics. Use Weekly Schedule mode to simulate a real game, or Custom Matchup mode to pit any two teams against each other. Choose 100 to 50,000 simulations, set which team is home or play on a neutral field, and pick a kickoff window. It runs on current projected 2026 rosters and real player usage, with opponent-adjusted team strength, player-level defense, and special teams built in.

How the NFL simulator works

Every matchup is simulated the number of times you choose (10,000 by default, up to 50,000). Each simulation plays out a sequence of drives, and each drive's outcome is drawn from real NFL drive data rather than a smooth bell curve. That distribution is tilted by each team's opponent-adjusted points-per-drive rating, so a strong offense facing a weak defense scores more, and a good defense drags an opponent down. Because points come from real drive outcomes, the simulated margins naturally cluster on the football key numbers of 3 and 7 instead of landing on impossible or unrealistic scores.

The win probability is not raw model output. It is calibrated against a held-out set of games, so a projection of 70 percent is tuned to reflect roughly a 70 percent real hit rate. Every run is shown with the exact simulation count used, and you can re-run it to explore how the range holds up.

Game environment: home field, neutral site, and kickoff window

You control the game environment. Assign either team home-field advantage or set a neutral field, and pick a kickoff window. These feed a small, transparent adjustment layer: home-field advantage estimated from the data, a modest travel adjustment when the visitor crosses time zones, and a small body-clock effect for early or night kickoffs after a long trip. Indoor and dome venues carry no weather effect. Every adjustment is bounded, and the "Why the model moved" section explains exactly how the environment you selected changed the projection.

What the simulation includes

  • Current projected 2026 rosters and player usage, so the quarterback, running backs, and receivers on the field, and how touches and targets are shared, reflect each team's current depth chart rather than last season.
  • Opponent-adjusted team strength, with offense and defense ratings solved jointly so a team's numbers account for the quality of who they played.
  • Projected team strength and quarterback impact, measured as the change from a team's baseline, so a quarterback change or a backup starting moves the score and win probability.
  • Player-level defense, aggregated from the projected defensive lineup, so pass rush, coverage, and run defense shape sacks, completions, and rushing.
  • Special teams, with kicker field-goal accuracy by distance, punting, and return threat reflected in the box score.
  • Home-field advantage, travel and kickoff-window effects estimated from the data, plus pace and drives per game, turnovers, and defensive scores.
  • A fully reconciled box score where passing, rushing, receiving, kicking, defense, and the scoring summary add up exactly to the team totals and the final score.

How to simulate a matchup

  1. Choose Weekly Schedule and tap a game, or choose Custom Matchup and pick any two teams.
  2. Set your simulation count, venue / home field, and kickoff window.
  3. Read the projected score, win probability, distribution charts, margin and total ranges, and the most likely final scores.
  4. Open the Box Score tab for full team and player statistics, and the Rosters & injuries tab to see expected starters and inactives.
  5. Use Simulate again to run a fresh variation with the same teams, count, venue and kickoff window.

Limitations and responsible use

This is a model projection, not a guarantee, and not betting or financial advice. A single NFL game carries a lot of variance, so single-game margins can miss by around a touchdown and a half even when the model is well calibrated, which is why every result is shown as a range. The projection reflects current projected rosters, so it shifts as depth charts, roles, and availability change. Use the output as one research input alongside lineups, weather, and market prices. NFL Simulator FAQ

What is the TrustMyRecord NFL Simulator?

It is a free NFL game simulator that plays out any matchup thousands of times, drive by drive, using current projected rosters, opponent-adjusted team strength, player-level defense, and special teams. Each run returns a projected final score, a calibrated win probability, and a full reconciled box score, so the output is a realistic range of outcomes for research rather than a single guess.

Can I choose how many simulations to run?

Yes. You can run 100, 1,000, 5,000, 10,000, 25,000 or 50,000 simulations. The default is 10,000. Every result, including the projected score, win probability, distributions and box score, is averaged across the number of simulations you select, and the exact count is shown on the result.

Can I simulate any two NFL teams?

Yes. Custom Matchup mode lets you pick any two NFL teams and simulate them with the same engine, rosters, injuries and box-score generation as scheduled games. You choose which team is home, or set a neutral field, and pick a kickoff window.

Does it use current NFL rosters?

Yes. Current mode is built on current projected 2026 rosters and depth charts, so the quarterback, running backs, receivers, defensive lineup, and specialists reflect who is expected to play now, and their real player usage drives how touches and targets are shared. A player who is out is excluded.

Does it generate player statistics and box scores?

Yes. Every simulation produces a full box score with realistic player statistics: passing, rushing, and receiving lines, defensive stats such as sacks and interceptions, kicking, and a play-by-play scoring summary. The box score is reconciled by construction, so passing, rushing, receiving, and scoring add up exactly to the team totals and the final score.

How accurate are the simulations?

Across a full season of held-out games the model's calibrated win probabilities beat a coin flip on Brier score and its projected scoring tracks actual scoring closely, but single-game margins carry roughly a touchdown-and-a-half of error because real NFL outcomes are highly variable. Results are shown as ranges, not guarantees, and it is not betting advice or a prediction of exact future results.

Is the NFL simulator free?

Yes. The NFL game simulator is free to use. You can simulate any matchup, review the projected score, win probability, player stats, and box score, and then track real, verified betting records separately on TrustMyRecord.
