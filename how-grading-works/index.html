How Pick Grading Works

Every record on TrustMyRecord is built by the same automated grading engine. Same scores, same math, same rules for everyone. Here is exactly what happens between the final whistle and the number on a profile.

Where results come from

Picks grade against the official final score of the game, pulled from the platform's live results feed. No human types in results, no capper reports their own outcome, and no user can dispute a score to flip a grade. If the game went final, the grade follows the score.

Grading runs automatically after each game finishes. Most picks settle shortly after the final score posts, and the result flows straight into the public record: wins, losses, unit profit, ROI, win percentage, and streaks all update together.

Win, loss, or push

Each graded pick lands in exactly one of three buckets:

ResultWhat it meansEffect on record
WonThe selection covered: the moneyline side won, the spread covered, or the total went the picked way.Counts as a win, adds unit profit.
LostThe selection did not cover.Counts as a loss, subtracts the risked units.
PushThe result landed exactly on the line (for example, a 3-point favorite wins by exactly 3).Settles at zero units. Not a win, not a loss.

Postponed or cancelled games void: the pick settles at zero units and drops out of the record the same way a push does, so nobody eats a loss on a game that never finished.

The exact unit math

Unit profit and loss is calculated from the odds locked at submission, the same way for every member:

FAVORITE (minus odds):  WIN = +units  |  LOSS = -units × (|odds| / 100)
UNDERDOG (plus odds):   WIN = +units × (odds / 100)  |  LOSS = -units
PUSH = 0

Example: 2 units on a -150 favorite returns +2.00u on a win and -3.00u on a loss. 2 units on a +130 underdog returns +2.60u on a win and -2.00u on a loss. Because the odds snapshot locks with the pick, line movement after submission never changes the math. The full return-on-investment calculation built on top of this is covered in ROI explained and the units guide.

Why pending picks never count

A record you can trust only counts finished business. Pending picks are excluded from the record, units, ROI, and win percentage until they settle. That closes the oldest trick in the pick-selling book: padding a "current run" with open bets that quietly disappear when they lose.

Combined with locked timestamps (no edits, no deletes, picks lock before the game starts), the grading engine makes a TrustMyRecord profile something you can audit, not something you have to take on faith.
