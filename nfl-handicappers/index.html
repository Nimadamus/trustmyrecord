Why NFL records are the easiest to fake

Cherry-picking thrives where games are scarce. An NFL handicapper might make a dozen plays a week for eighteen weeks, so a couple of green Sundays manufacture an "11-4 on the year" claim while the quiet losers disappear. That is the entire reason this board exists: every NFL pick is locked before kickoff and graded automatically, so the bad-beat backdoor covers and the totals that landed dead on the number all stay on the record next to the wins. Over a short season, that honesty is the whole game.

Closing line value is the real signal in football

Because the NFL moves so hard on practice reports, Friday designations, Sunday inactives and weather, the sharpest measure of a capper is not the scoreboard, it is whether they beat the number. A record that locks a price before the move captures closing line value on its own: did they take +3 before it closed +2.5, or chase it to +2? Read the leaders here through that lens, alongside units and ROI.
