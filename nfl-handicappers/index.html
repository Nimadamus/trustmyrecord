How the NFL rankings work

TrustMyRecord ranks NFL handicappers using their verified record. When a member logs a pick, it locks with a timestamp before the game and is auto-graded against the final result, updating net units, ROI, average odds, win rate, and streak. Featured rankings require at least 20 graded picks and reward profitability and consistency over volume alone, so a single hot week cannot dominate the board.

Read the leaderboard the right way

Sort by units to see who has produced the most profit, and by ROI to see who is most efficient with what they risk. Always check sample size: a +5 unit record over 200 picks is far more meaningful than the same number over 10. Be skeptical of records built only on heavy favorites, because a high win rate can still lose money.

Frequently asked questions

How are NFL handicappers ranked?
Rankings are based on verified, auto-graded picks. We weight net units, ROI, and recent activity, and require at least 20 graded picks for featured placement so a tiny lucky sample cannot top the board.
Are these records real?
Yes. Every pick is locked with a timestamp before the event and graded automatically against the final result. Records are not self-reported and cannot be edited after the fact.
Do you sell NFL picks?
No. TrustMyRecord does not sell picks, does not take wagers, and is not a sportsbook. It is a free verification and leaderboard platform.
What is the best metric to judge a handicapper?
A positive unit and ROI figure over a large sample size is the strongest signal. Win percentage on its own is misleading.
