The NBA bet menu

Spreads and moneylines

The point spread is the headline NBA market, and the half-point around key margins matters. The moneyline is the straight winner, where road dogs and blowout-prone favorites both live. Both grade off the final score.

Totals and team totals

Game over/under and single-team totals, the markets most exposed to pace and rest. A fast-pace matchup or a blowout that empties the benches can swing a total ten points, so the number you locked is frozen before tip.

Quarters, halves and alternate lines

First-half and first-quarter spreads and totals, plus alternate spreads and totals at adjusted prices. The tracker settles each on the relevant period, not just the final.

An ocean of NBA props

No sport offers more player props than the NBA, and TrustMyRecord grades the full board: points, rebounds, assists, threes made, steals, blocks and turnovers, double-doubles and triple-doubles, and the combo lines, points-rebounds-assists (PRA), points plus rebounds, and the rest. Props are also where the late-scratch trap bites hardest, so each one is tied to the player and number you locked. If a star is ruled out at warmups and a backup's line explodes, your graded prop reflects the bet you actually made.

Rest, pace and the schedule spots that decide games

NBA edges come from the things the box score only confirms after the fact: a team on the second night of a back-to-back, a pace mismatch that inflates a total, a quiet rest day for a contender, a star shut down for "injury management." Because your number is locked ahead of those moves, the record shows whether you read the spot or reacted to it, and your average odds reveal whether you are consistently early or consistently chasing.

Frequently asked questions

What happens to my pick if a star is scratched at warmups?
Your pick is frozen with the spread or total and timestamp from when you logged it. A late scratch that moves the line after lock cannot be used to claim a better number, so a game-time injury report can never quietly improve your record.
Which NBA markets can I track?
Point spreads, moneylines, game totals, team totals, first-half and quarter lines, alternate spreads, and player props such as points, rebounds, assists, threes made and the points-rebounds-assists combos. Each locks before tip and auto-grades on the final box score.
Why do timestamps matter so much in the NBA?
NBA lines move faster than almost any sport. A single load-management announcement or a back-to-back rest decision can swing a total several points in minutes. The timestamp proves you took your number before that move, not after.
How are back-to-backs and rest factored in?
The tracker does not predict for you, it verifies. But because every pick is timestamped, your record honestly reflects whether you anticipated rest, pace and back-to-back spots ahead of the market or reacted to news after the line had already moved.
