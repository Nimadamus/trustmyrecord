How MLB The Show competition works on TrustMyRecord

TrustMyRecord built its reputation on one idea: records you can actually trust. The Arena applies that same standard to sports gaming. When you play an MLB The Show challenge through the Arena, the final score is reported by one player and must be confirmed by the opponent, or reviewed and verified by an admin, before it counts. Confirmed results feed your public gaming record, your head-to-head rivalries, and league standings, so your profile shows a verified competitive history instead of empty trash talk.

Head-to-head challenges

Create a direct challenge against a specific player or post an open challenge anyone can accept. Set the console, schedule, series length, and house rules up front. Disputed results go to admin review with screenshot proof.

Online leagues and standings

Leagues run on a round-robin schedule with public standings computed from confirmed results only: wins, losses, run differential. Start your own league, set the player cap and season start date, and invite the community.

Free predictions with Arena Points

Everyone gets 1,000 free Arena Points to start. Use them to make free predictions, we call them Arena Picks, on other players' matches before they start. Call the winner correctly and earn more points; miss and you lose what you entered. Predictions lock at match start, you can never predict your own match, and prediction records are tracked separately from sports pick records. Arena Points are purely for fun: they have no cash value, cannot be purchased, and cannot be withdrawn.
