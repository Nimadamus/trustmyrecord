How the MLB Run Prediction Model Works

Each model run projects team run totals and a predicted final score for every MLB matchup on the slate. The projections are built from four factor groups per team: offense against the opposing starter's handedness (lineup wRC+, wOBA, and ISO versus that hand), the opposing starter's underlying skill (FIP and K-BB rate rather than surface ERA), opposing bullpen condition (30-day FIP plus recent pitch workload and high-leverage availability), and recent team form (runs per game over the last 14 days). A park factor adjusts the run environment for the venue.

Every card shows projected runs and win probability for both teams, the market total for context, and a plain-language explanation of which factors drove the number. The backtest panel above the cards reports the model's historical out-of-sample accuracy: team-run mean absolute error, winner accuracy, exact final-score hit rate, and total-direction win rate at a one-run edge. These projections are a research view of the matchup, not betting advice, and the page states its data limitations directly on each run.
