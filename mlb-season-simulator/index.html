What Is an MLB Season Simulator?

An MLB season simulator is a way to play out a full baseball season before, or while, it happens. Instead of guessing one game at a time, you work through the whole picture: which teams win each division, which clubs grab the Wild Card spots, who wins each pennant, who lifts the World Series trophy, and how many games the contenders win across a 162-game grind.

Think of it as a baseball season simulator, MLB standings simulator, and MLB playoff simulator rolled into one. You set the final standings, build the October bracket, and predict win totals, all on a single page. It doubles as an MLB record predictor: every win-total call you make is graded against the real 162-game results.

This page puts you in the simulator's seat. You think through the matchups, the rosters, the schedule, and the storylines, then lock in your own season outlook. The builder above is live: pick your teams, submit while logged in, and your entry is saved to your account and timestamped so it counts.

Want a computer to crunch a single matchup with win probabilities instead? Use the separate MLB game simulator, then come back here to lock your season-long calls.

MLB Season Simulator FAQ

Can I submit MLB season predictions on this page?

Yes. Log in to TrustMyRecord, pick all six division winners, the AL and NL wild cards, both pennant winners, the World Series champion, and projected win totals, then lock your entry. Your predictions are saved to your account and timestamped.

How are my season predictions verified?

When you lock your entry the server records a timestamp and ties it to your public profile before results are known. At season's end your division winners, playoff teams, champions, and win totals can be graded against real outcomes, so your accuracy is provable.

Can I change my predictions after I lock them?

No. You can save a draft and edit it freely, but once you lock your entry it is frozen and timestamped. No deleting losers, no quiet edits. That is the whole point of a verified record.

Is this an automated simulation engine?

This page is a prediction builder where you make the calls, submit them, and lock them for grading. To run a single computer-simulated matchup with win probabilities, use the separate MLB Simulator.

Can I simulate the MLB standings and playoff bracket?

Yes. Setting all six division winners builds your projected MLB standings, and choosing the Wild Cards, pennant winners, and World Series champion fills out a full playoff bracket. It works as both an MLB standings simulator and an MLB playoff simulator in one place.

Can I predict each team's win total?

Yes. Step 4 lets you enter a regular-season win total from 0 to 162 for any team you want graded. That turns the tool into an MLB record predictor, with every win-total call checked against the real standings at the end of the year.

Is the MLB season simulator free?

Yes. Building, saving, and locking your season predictions is completely free. All you need is a free TrustMyRecord account to submit an entry and put it on your public record.
