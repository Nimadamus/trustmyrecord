DMCA Copyright Policy

TrustMyRecord respects the intellectual property rights of others and expects every member to do the same. This page explains how to report copyright infringement on any part of the platform, including the forum, user profiles, and Arena live streams and replays, and how to respond if your content is removed.

Reporting Copyright Infringement

If you believe content hosted on TrustMyRecord infringes a copyright you own or control, you may submit a takedown notice under the Digital Millennium Copyright Act (17 U.S.C. § 512). When we receive a valid notice, we act expeditiously to remove or disable access to the identified material, including terminating an active live stream when the notice concerns a stream in progress.

How to Submit a Takedown Notice

Send your notice to our designated agent at support@trustmyrecord.com with the subject line "DMCA Takedown Notice", or through the contact page. To be valid under the DMCA, your notice must include all of the following:

1. A physical or electronic signature of the copyright owner or a person authorized to act on the owner's behalf.

2. Identification of the copyrighted work claimed to have been infringed (or, if multiple works are covered by a single notice, a representative list).

3. Identification of the material claimed to be infringing, with enough information for us to locate it, for example the full URL of the stream, replay, post, or page.

4. Your contact information: name, mailing address, telephone number, and email address.

5. A statement that you have a good-faith belief that use of the material in the manner complained of is not authorized by the copyright owner, its agent, or the law.

6. A statement, made under penalty of perjury, that the information in the notice is accurate and that you are the copyright owner or authorized to act on the owner's behalf.

Incomplete notices may not be actionable. Knowingly misrepresenting that material is infringing can expose you to liability under 17 U.S.C. § 512(f).

Counter-Notices

If your content was removed and you believe the removal was a mistake or misidentification, you may submit a counter-notice to the same designated agent. A valid counter-notice must include: your physical or electronic signature; identification of the removed material and the location where it appeared before removal; a statement under penalty of perjury that you have a good-faith belief the material was removed as a result of mistake or misidentification; your name, address, and telephone number; and a statement that you consent to the jurisdiction of the federal district court for your judicial district (or, if outside the United States, any judicial district in which TrustMyRecord may be found) and that you will accept service of process from the person who filed the original notice.

If we receive a valid counter-notice, we may restore the removed material in 10 to 14 business days unless the original complainant notifies us that they have filed a court action seeking to restrain the alleged infringement.

Live Streams and Replays

Arena streaming on TrustMyRecord is limited to members' own live gameplay of supported titles with their own commentary. Rebroadcasting copyrighted content such as television broadcasts, other people's streams, pay-per-view events, or music the streamer does not have rights to is prohibited by our streaming policy before any DMCA notice is ever involved. Valid takedown notices against a live stream result in the stream being ended and, where appropriate, the associated replay being removed.

Repeat Infringer Policy

TrustMyRecord terminates, in appropriate circumstances, the accounts and streaming privileges of members who are repeat infringers. Streaming access is a privilege, not a right, and a single serious violation can end it.
