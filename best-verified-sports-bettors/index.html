How to read a bettor's record

Five checks that take two minutes and filter out almost everyone.

Sample size

Dozens of picks prove nothing. Look for hundreds of graded picks before the numbers mean much.

Net units, not win rate

A 58 percent record on big favorites can lose money. Units at recorded odds are the truth.

ROI per pick

Return on total risked shows efficiency. Sustained positive ROI over volume is rare and valuable.

Visible losses

If you cannot find the losing picks, the record is curated. Walk away.

Timestamps

Picks must be locked before the games. Anything posted after the fact is content, not a record.

Consistency over streaks

Anyone can run hot for a month. Look at the full curve, not the best stretch.

Red flags that expose fake cappers

The patterns repeat everywhere: win percentages quoted with no odds attached, screenshots instead of ledgers, records that restart whenever a cold streak hits, and streak claims with no timestamped trail. Verified bettors FAQ

Who are the best verified sports bettors?

The ones whose full graded records are public and hold up. TrustMyRecord ranks verified net units and ROI on public leaderboards, so the standings are visible, not claimed.

What should I check before trusting a record?

Sample size, net units, ROI, average odds, and visible losses. A short or curated history proves very little.

What are the red flags?

Win rates quoted without odds, screenshots instead of ledgers, deleted history, and streak claims with no timestamps.

Does a high win rate mean someone is good?

No. Heavy favorites win often and can still lose money. Net units and ROI at the recorded odds are what matter. See ROI explained.
