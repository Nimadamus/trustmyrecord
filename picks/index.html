What "Making Picks" Means Here

On most sports apps a pick is a screenshot. On TrustMyRecord, a pick is a locked submission. You enter the side, line, and units before a game starts, the entry is timestamped, and it stays attached to your username forever. When the game finishes, the result grades automatically and your record updates in public.

The point is not to beat the book in private. The point is to build a sports reputation with receipts: a record sharp bettors, casual fans, and skeptics can all read the same way.

Why "Public" Matters

Sports credibility online is mostly built on screenshots and selective memory. Bios get rewritten, bad weeks get muted, winning weeks get cropped and reposted. A locked, public record makes that impossible. If you say you are sharp, the ledger says it for you. If you go cold, the ledger says that too. Honesty is the product.

That accountability is the entire reason TrustMyRecord exists. Every other feature on the site, the sportsbook, the leaderboards, the polls, the marketplace, the gaming arena, all sit on top of this one rule: once you lock a pick, you cannot rewrite the receipt.
