NFL markets you can track

Football betting is a spread-first game, and the half-point matters. Log point spreads, moneylines, game totals, team totals, first-half and quarter lines, alternate spreads, and player props from passing and rushing yards to receptions and anytime touchdowns. The instant you log a pick, TrustMyRecord stores the exact number and price and stamps it before kickoff. That timestamp is the whole point: a -3 you took Tuesday stays -3 on your record even after the key number moves to -3.5 by Sunday.

When the game goes final, the auto-grader reads the official score, applies the spread or total you locked, and settles the pick automatically, then updates your net units, ROI, average odds, win rate and current streak. A push on the number grades as a push. Nothing gets re-priced after the result is known.

Why verified NFL records matter more than any other sport

Cherry-picking is easiest where games are scarcest. An NFL handicapper makes maybe a dozen plays a week for 18 weeks, so it only takes a couple of lucky Sundays to manufacture a "we're 11-4 on the year" claim while the quiet losers vanish. Locked, auto-graded picks make that impossible. Every loss, every bad beat on a late field goal, every total that landed dead on the number stays on the page next to the wins, graded the same way every time. Over a small NFL sample, that honesty is the difference between a real edge and variance dressed up as skill.

Line movement, weather, late news and closing line value

No sport moves on information like the NFL. Wednesday practice reports, Friday designations, Sunday-morning inactives and a forecast of 25 mph wind swing spreads and totals before a snap is played, and good handicappers are graded on whether they beat the market, not just the result. Because TrustMyRecord freezes your number before that move, your record naturally captures closing line value: did you take +3 before it closed +2.5, or chase it down to +2? Units measure profit independent of stake, ROI shows return per unit risked, and sample size tells you how much an 11-4 start is really worth.

It records the exact spread, total or price you locked at kickoff, with a timestamp. Because that number is frozen before the market moves, your record reflects the line you actually got, which is the only fair way to judge closing line value over a season.
Which NFL markets can I track?

Why does an 18-week sample matter so much in the NFL?
With only 17 games per team, an NFL bettor makes a fraction of the picks a baseball or basketball bettor makes. That tiny sample is exactly why a few cherry-picked winners look impressive, and why a locked, full-season record is the only honest measure.
