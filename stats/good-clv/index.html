What Is a Good CLV?

Good CLV means your bets consistently beat the closing line. Any steady positive average over a real sample is good, and it is rarer than most bettors think.

('

What good CLV actually looks like

Good CLV is simpler than people expect: your bets, on average, get a better price than the number the market closes at. There is no universal threshold, because value differs by sport and market, but the principle holds everywhere. A bettor who lands +150 on teams that close +120, or -2.5 on games that close -3.5, again and again, has good CLV even if their win percentage looks ordinary.

The bar is lower than most think and harder than most expect. You do not need to crush the close. A small, consistent positive average, held across a large sample, is genuinely good, because beating the sharpest price of the day even slightly is not something variance produces.

', '

Why even a thin edge is good

The closing line already reflects nearly everything knowable about a game. Beating it means you priced the game before the market did. Do that by even a hair per bet and it compounds: the same skill that produced good CLV this month is the skill that produces positive ROI and net units over a season. That is why sharp bettors treat good CLV as the leading indicator and results as the lagging one.

Context still matters. Good CLV on ten picks means little; good CLV on many hundreds is a real signal. Read it next to the CLV meaning and against its opposite, bad CLV, to see the full picture.

The trap in judging good CLV

A hot record with unknown CLV is not proof of skill, and a cold record with good CLV is not proof of failure. The bettors worth following are the ones whose prices consistently beat the close, because that is the record that tends to turn profitable. When you scan verified handicappers, weigh the quality of the numbers they take, not just the wins they show.

Frequently asked questions

What is a good CLV number?

There is no single magic figure, but a consistently positive average CLV over a large sample is good. Even a small steady edge over the close, held over hundreds of picks, marks a skilled bettor.

Is 2 percent CLV good?

A steady positive CLV of a couple percent across a real sample is very good and hard to sustain. The key word is steady: one lucky stretch of positive CLV is not the same as a durable edge.

Can good CLV come with a losing record?

Over a short window, yes. Good CLV with a flat or slightly negative record is often a bettor whose results have not caught up to their prices yet.
