CLV Betting Meaning

CLV stands for closing line value. In betting, it means the difference between the price you locked and the price the market closed at on the same bet.

('

The plain-English meaning

CLV is short for closing line value. The closing line is the final price a market shows right before it stops taking action. CLV is simply how your price compares to that closing price. If you bet a team at +150 and it closes at +120, you got value the rest of the market never saw, so your CLV is positive. If you bet -3.5 and it closes -2.5, the market moved your way after you were already on the worse number, so your CLV is negative.

The reason bettors obsess over it: the closing line is the sharpest number of the day. It has absorbed every injury report, every model, and every dollar of sharp money. Consistently beating it is not something luck produces, which is why positive CLV is the classic signal of a skilled bettor. See the fuller breakdown on the closing line value page.

', '

How CLV is measured

CLV = the price you locked minus the price the market closed at, on the same market

A single pick tells you very little. CLV means something across a large sample: average it over hundreds of graded picks and it separates bettors who find value before the market from bettors who chase it. That is why CLV is read alongside units, ROI, and win percentage, never on its own.

', '

What counts as good and bad CLV

Once you understand the meaning, the next question is where the line sits. A positive average that holds up over a real sample is good CLV. A negative average that persists is bad CLV, and it is the quiet reason many hot records eventually collapse. Those two pages break down the benchmarks.

Why the meaning matters more than the result

The whole point of CLV is that it strips out luck. A record can look great for months on bad prices and still be underwater in the long run, because the closing line was right and the bettor was late. When you evaluate a verified handicapper, the CLV meaning is the lens: are they getting numbers the market later agrees with, or just getting lucky on numbers the market already beat?

Frequently asked questions

What does CLV mean in betting?

CLV means closing line value, the gap between the odds you bet and the odds the same market closed at. Beating the close consistently is treated as evidence of skill.

Is CLV the same as winning?

No. CLV measures the price you got, not the result. You can win a bet with negative CLV or lose one with positive CLV. Over a long sample CLV predicts profit better than short-term results.

How do you read a CLV number?

Positive CLV means you took a better price than the close. Negative CLV means the market moved against your number. It is usually expressed as an average across many picks.
