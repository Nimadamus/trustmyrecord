Betting Units Explained

A unit is a standard measure of bet size, so a betting record can be judged by how much was risked and won instead of dollar amounts.

Why units matter

Dollar amounts hide more than they reveal. A bettor bragging about a $5,000 month might be risking $2,000 a game, while another bettor up $500 might be risking $20 a game and performing far better. Units strip out bankroll size so two records can be compared directly. One unit simply means one standard bet, and a record expressed in net units tells you how much a bettor has actually gained or lost relative to what they risked.

Units also force discipline into a public record. When every pick carries a stated unit size, a bettor cannot quietly double down after losses or shrink stakes after wins without it showing in the ledger.

How TrustMyRecord uses units

Every pick locked on TrustMyRecord carries a unit size, and every graded pick settles to a net unit result at the odds that were recorded when the pick locked. Those results add up to the net units figure shown on every profile, the handicapper directory, and the leaderboard, where members can be sorted by units directly. Contest standings in TrustMyRecord contests are also ranked by net units, not by win count.

Because picks cannot be edited or deleted after they lock, the units total reflects every bet a member ever graded, not a curated selection.

The calculation

Favorite (minus odds): win = +units risked  |  loss = −units × (|odds| ÷ 100)
Underdog (plus odds): win = +units × (odds ÷ 100)  |  loss = −units risked  |  push = 0

A 1 unit pick at -110 returns +0.91 units on a win and -1.00 on a loss. A 1 unit pick at +150 returns +1.50 units on a win and -1.00 on a loss. Net units is simply the running sum of those results across every graded pick.

A quick example

A member locks three 1 unit picks: a -110 spread that wins (+0.91), a +140 underdog that wins (+1.40), and a -150 favorite that loses (-1.50). Net result: +0.81 units on 3 units risked. Their record reads 2-1, but the units column tells the real story, and that is the number the leaderboard sorts by.

The common mistake

Treating a winning record as a winning bettor. A 10-5 record built on heavy favorites can easily be negative units, because favorite losses cost more than favorite wins pay. This is why TrustMyRecord leads with net units and ROI instead of raw win percentage, and why the featured leaders on the handicapper directory must show positive net units, not just a winning record.

Frequently asked questions

What is a unit in sports betting?

A standard measure of bet size. One unit is one standard bet, so records can be compared across bettors regardless of bankroll. On TrustMyRecord every locked pick carries a unit size and grades to a net unit result.

How are net units calculated?

Each graded pick settles at the odds recorded when it locked, using the standard favorite and underdog payout math. Net units is the running sum across every graded pick. Pushes count as zero.

Why do leaderboards rank by units instead of wins?

Win counts ignore odds and bet size. Net units accounts for both, so it measures actual profit. That is why the TrustMyRecord leaderboard and contests rank by net units.

Can pending picks change my units total?

No. Pending picks are excluded from units, ROI, and streaks until the autograder settles them from final scores. See the pending picks guide for details.
