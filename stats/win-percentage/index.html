Win Percentage Explained

Win percentage is the share of a bettor's graded picks that won, and on its own it says nothing about profit.

The formula

Win percentage = wins ÷ graded picks × 100

On TrustMyRecord, only graded picks count. Pending picks are excluded entirely, and pushes are tracked separately in the W-L-P record rather than counted as wins.

Why it matters, and why it lies

Win percentage is the most quoted stat in sports betting and the most abused. It matters because, at a known set of odds, it maps directly to profitability: at standard -110 juice, the break-even point is about 52.4%. Above that, a spread bettor profits; below it, they lose.

The problem is that win percentage ignores odds. A bettor hammering -250 favorites can hit 65% and bleed units. An underdog specialist can hit 45% and print them. Quoting a win rate without the average odds behind it is how bad records get marketed as good ones.

How TrustMyRecord uses it

Win percentage appears on every profile and in the handicapper directory, where it is one of the sortable columns alongside units and ROI. It is always presented next to the full W-L-P record and graded pick count, never alone. Leaderboards and contests rank by net units rather than win rate, precisely because win rate alone rewards the wrong behavior.

The common mistake

Tailing the highest win percentage on the board. A 70% capper with 10 graded picks is a coin flip away from 60%, and a 70% capper on heavy favorites may be losing money outright. Read win percentage with three companions: sample size, average odds, and ROI. On TrustMyRecord all four sit on the same profile, computed from the same locked ledger.

Frequently asked questions

What is a good win percentage in sports betting?

It depends on the odds. At -110, break-even is about 52.4%, so anything sustained above that profits. At -200 the bar is 66.7%, and dog bettors can profit below 50%.

What win percentage do you need to break even at -110?

About 52.4%. You risk 1.10 to win 1.00, so the wins must cover the juice on the losses.

Why does TrustMyRecord rank by units instead of win percentage?

Win percentage ignores odds and can reward unprofitable betting. Net units measures actual profit at the recorded odds, so leaderboards and contests rank by units.

Do pushes count against win percentage?

Pushes are tracked separately in the W-L-P record. They settle at zero units and do not count as wins.
