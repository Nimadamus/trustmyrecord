Betting ROI on TrustMyRecord

ROI shows how much profit or loss a bettor has generated compared to the total amount risked.

Why ROI matters

ROI is the efficiency number. Two bettors can both be up 10 units, but if one risked 100 units to get there and the other risked 400, they are not the same bettor. ROI divides profit by total risk, which makes records of different sizes and styles directly comparable. It is the single hardest stat to fake over volume, because every losing pick drags it down at the exact odds that pick carried.

For a deeper general primer on reading ROI, benchmarks, and worked examples at different odds, see our full guide: Sports Betting ROI Explained. This page covers how the number is actually produced on TrustMyRecord.

How TrustMyRecord computes it

ROI = (net units won ÷ total units risked) × 100

The inputs come straight from the pick ledger:

  • Net units won is the sum of every graded pick's unit result at the odds recorded when the pick locked. See how unit results are calculated.
  • Total units risked is the sum of unit sizes across all graded picks.
  • Pending picks are excluded. ROI only moves when the autograder settles a pick from final scores.
  • Pushes count zero profit but still count as risk, so a heavy-push record will not inflate its own ROI.

The resulting figure appears on every profile's Advanced Capper Metrics strip, on the handicapper directory where members can be sorted by ROI, and on the leaderboard.

A quick example

A member grades 50 picks at 1 unit each, finishing 27-22-1 with a mix of favorites and dogs that nets +3.4 units. Total risked: 50 units. ROI: 3.4 ÷ 50 × 100 = +6.8%. That is a strong figure if it holds over hundreds of picks, and a meaningless one if it stops at 50, which is why ROI should always be read next to sample size.

The common mistake

Trusting a big ROI on a small record. Variance alone routinely produces +30% ROI over 20 picks; almost nothing produces it over 500. TrustMyRecord shows graded pick counts beside ROI everywhere, and profiles will show "Not enough data yet" rather than display an ROI built on a sample too small to mean anything. Picks lock before game time and cannot be edited or deleted, so the ROI you see is the ROI that happened. That is the standard the platform rules exist to protect.

Frequently asked questions

How does TrustMyRecord calculate ROI?

Net units won divided by total units risked, times 100, computed from the locked pick ledger. Pending picks are excluded; pushes count as risk with zero profit.

Why is my ROI showing as not enough data?

Profile metrics require a minimum number of graded picks before they display, because ROI over a handful of picks is noise. Grade more picks and the figure appears.

Is ROI or net units more important?

They answer different questions. Units measure the size of the profit; ROI measures efficiency relative to risk. Read both, alongside graded volume.

Can a bettor edit picks to improve ROI?

No. Picks lock at submission and cannot be edited or deleted, so ROI reflects every pick ever graded. That is the core of the platform's pick integrity rule.

What is a realistic long-term betting ROI?

Sustained positive ROI over hundreds of graded picks is uncommon. Single digits held over real volume is strong; spectacular figures usually mean small samples. See the full ROI guide for benchmarks.
