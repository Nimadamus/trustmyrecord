Sports Betting Stats Explained

The metrics TrustMyRecord uses to evaluate verified betting records, and how to read them without being fooled.

TrustMyRecord tracks more than wins and losses. A serious betting record needs context: units, ROI, odds, sample size, streaks, graded picks, pending picks, and sport-specific performance. These guides explain how each stat works, how it is computed on this platform, and why it matters when you evaluate a public betting record.

Every number on a TrustMyRecord profile comes from the same place: picks that locked before game time, with odds and unit sizes recorded, graded automatically from final scores, with no edits and no deletions. The stats below are how that ledger gets read.

Core record stats

The numbers on every profile, directory row, and leaderboard. These measure what a record actually earned.

Units

The standard measure of bet size and profit. Net units is how leaderboards and contests rank every record.

ROI

Net units won divided by total units risked. The efficiency stat that makes records of any size comparable.

Win Percentage

The share of graded picks that won, and why it means nothing without odds and volume beside it.

Average Odds

The typical price a bettor takes. The context that decides what win percentage a record needs to profit.

Graded Picks

Locked picks settled from final scores by the autograder. The unit of truth in every verified record.

Pending Picks

Locked, public, timestamped, and worth zero until graded. The proof-of-timing layer of the record.

Advanced evaluation stats

The second layer of reading a record: whether the headline numbers are signal or noise.

Sample Size

The graded volume behind a record decides whether it is evidence or luck. Includes the thresholds TrustMyRecord enforces.

Closing Line Value

Whether a bettor beats the market's sharpest price. The classic skill test, made checkable by locked odds and timestamps.

CLV Betting Meaning

What CLV stands for and how to read a plus or minus number, in plain English.

Good CLV

What counts as good closing line value, and why even a thin steady edge over the close is strong.

Bad CLV

Why consistently negative CLV quietly sinks a record even while the wins pile up.

Streaks

Runs of consecutive wins or losses. What they prove on a verified record, and what they never predict.

Pick Diversity

Unique opinions versus repeated exposure. How many independent judgments a record actually tests.

Z-Score

How many standard deviations a record sits above break-even. The skill-versus-luck number.

Variance

Why records swing, how big normal downswings are, and why short samples prove nothing.

Frequently asked questions

What stats does TrustMyRecord track on a betting record?

W-L-P, net units, ROI, win percentage, average odds, streaks, graded and pending counts, sport splits, and advanced metrics like Pick Diversity, all computed from locked picks.

Which stat matters most when evaluating a bettor?

No single one. Units and ROI measure profit, sample size decides whether they mean anything, and average odds gives win percentage its context. Read them together.

Why can TrustMyRecord stats be trusted?

Picks lock before game time, cannot be edited or deleted, and are graded automatically from final scores. Stats are computed from that ledger, not self-reported.

Do pending picks affect these stats?

No. Pending picks are excluded from records, units, ROI, and streaks until the autograder settles them.
