Pending Picks Explained

A pending pick is locked and public but not yet graded, so it counts toward nothing until the game ends.

Why pending picks matter

Pending picks are the proof-of-timing layer of a verified record. A pick sitting in pending status is a public, timestamped commitment: this member took this side, at these odds, before the game started. That is what separates a real record from a screenshot posted after the final score.

Just as important is what pending picks do not do: they do not count. A record, a win percentage, an ROI figure, or a streak that included ungraded picks would be partly fictional, because nothing has been risked and resolved yet.

How TrustMyRecord handles them

The rules are strict and simple:

  • A pick locks at submission with its market, line, odds, and units, and cannot be edited or deleted while pending. That is Rule 2, Pick Integrity.
  • Pending picks are excluded from W-L-P, units, ROI, and streaks. Profile stats read only from graded results.
  • When the game ends, the autograder settles the pick and it becomes a graded pick, joining the record at that moment.
  • In contests, picks are sealed until first pitch, then visible like any locked pick, and contest results stay isolated from the main record.

A quick example

A member with a 120-100 record and +9.4 units locks five picks for tonight. Until those games end, their profile still reads 120-100 and +9.4. If the slate goes 3-2, the autograder writes the results and the record becomes 123-102 with whatever the locked odds paid. At no point did the pending slate flatter or damage the numbers; it simply waited its turn.

The common mistake

Treating pending volume as performance. "I have 30 picks in play" tells you about activity, not skill, and any platform that lets ungraded picks touch a record is advertising a number that has not happened. The companion mistake is wanting losers scrubbed before they grade; a record that can drop its pending losers is not a record. On TrustMyRecord pending picks are visible, immovable, and worth exactly zero until the autograder says otherwise.

Frequently asked questions

Do pending picks affect my record on TrustMyRecord?

No. Pending picks are excluded from W-L-P, units, ROI, and streaks until the autograder settles them from final scores.

Can I edit or delete a pending pick?

No. Picks lock at submission and cannot be edited or deleted. That restriction is what makes every record verifiable.

When does a pending pick become graded?

After the game finishes, the autograder settles it from the final score as a win, loss, or push at the locked odds.

Are contest picks pending in the same way?

Contest picks are sealed until first pitch and graded the same way, but tracked separately in Contest Mode, never mixing with your main record.

Locked now, graded later

Lock your picks before game time and watch them move from pending to graded automatically. The record writes itself.
