What Is Bad CLV?

Bad CLV means your bets consistently take a worse price than the market closes at. It is the quiet reason many hot-looking records eventually collapse.

('

What bad CLV actually means

Bad CLV is the mirror image of good CLV. It means that, on average, the price you locked was worse than the price the market closed at. You bet -3.5 and it closes -2.5. You take +120 and it closes +150. The line moved away from your number after you were already committed, which is the market telling you it disagreed with your timing.

The danger is that bad CLV hides behind wins. A bettor can run hot for weeks on bad prices, because short-run variance overwhelms the small per-bet disadvantage. The prices, not the record, are the tell. Negative CLV plus a winning streak is a record living on borrowed time.

', '

Why bad CLV sinks a record

The closing line is the sharpest number of the day. Consistently taking a worse number than the close means paying a tax on every bet, and that tax compounds against ROI and net units exactly the way a small edge compounds for a sharp bettor. This is why a strong-looking win percentage with negative CLV is a warning sign, not a green light.

Common causes are all forms of being late: chasing steam after it has already moved the line, betting public favorites at inflated prices, and reacting to news the market already priced in. Fixing bad CLV means getting to a number before the market does, not after. Start with the CLV meaning to see how the number is read.

Frequently asked questions

What does bad CLV mean?

Bad CLV means you consistently bet worse prices than the closing line. The market moves against your number after you bet, which signals you are behind the market rather than ahead of it.

Can you win with bad CLV?

Yes, over a short sample. Variance lets bettors win for a while on bad prices. Over a long sample the closing line is usually right and negative CLV catches up with the record.

What causes bad CLV?

Betting late into moved lines, chasing steam after it has already hit, and taking public sides at inflated prices are common causes. It usually means following the market instead of leading it.

How do I know if my CLV is bad?
