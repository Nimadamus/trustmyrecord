Closing Line Value Explained

Closing line value measures whether a bettor consistently got a better price than the line the market closed at.

Why CLV matters

The closing line is the sharpest line of the day: it has absorbed every bet, every injury report, and every model the market has. Beating it consistently, taking -2.5 on a game that closes -3.5, or +150 on a team that closes +120, is widely treated as the strongest available evidence of real betting skill, often before results themselves confirm it.

The logic is simple. Results over a short window are mostly variance, but consistently getting better prices than the close is not something luck does. A bettor with positive CLV and a flat record is usually ahead of a bettor with a hot record and negative CLV.

The calculation

CLV = difference between the odds you locked and the closing odds on the same market

If you take +150 and the line closes +120, you beat the close. If you take -3.5 and it closes -2.5, the market moved against your number and your CLV on that pick is negative. Aggregated across hundreds of picks, the average tells you whether a bettor finds value before the market does or simply follows it.

The common mistake

Confusing winning with beating the market. A bettor can run hot for months with negative CLV, paying worse prices than the close on every play, and the market eventually collects. The reverse mistake is dismissing a bettor on a cold stretch whose numbers consistently beat the close; that is usually the record that turns. When you evaluate a verified handicapper, weigh the prices they take, not just the win percentage they show.

Frequently asked questions

What is closing line value in sports betting?

The difference between the odds you took and the closing odds on the same market. Consistently beating the close is strong evidence of real skill.

Why is CLV considered better evidence than results?

Short-term results are dominated by variance; consistently getting better prices than the close is not. Positive CLV tends to predict long-term profit.

Does TrustMyRecord show a CLV stat?

A CLV card is planned for profile Advanced Capper Metrics and displays once enough graded line data is available. Locked odds and timestamps already make records CLV-checkable.

Can you have positive CLV and still lose?

Over a short sample, yes. Over a long sample it is rare, which is why CLV paired with volume is so respected.
