MLB Simulator

How the simulator workflow is structured.

MLB Simulator is built around team selection, starter context, simulation inputs, and projected box score style output. This page explains the workflow without changing simulator behavior.

Step 1Select teams
Step 2Set starters
Step 3Run matchup
Step 4Review output

Step 1 starts with real rosters: pick any two MLB teams and the simulator loads their actual lineups. Step 2 sets the pitching matchup - choose each side's starting pitcher, which drives strikeout, walk, and contact rates for every simulated plate appearance. Step 3 runs the game: the engine simulates each half-inning batter by batter, including hits, home runs, walks, hit-by-pitches, sacrifice plays, bullpen changes, and scoring sequences. Step 4 presents the output as a full box score with line score, per-player batting lines, pitching lines, and a scoring log that explains how every run crossed the plate.

Because games are simulated play by play rather than drawn from a single win-probability number, no two runs are identical - close matchups produce split outcomes across repeated runs, and lopsided ones show it in the run distribution. The simulator route remains the source of truth for available teams, starter inputs, and generated output. No simulator logic is duplicated on this page.

Related tools: project a full season with the MLB Season Simulator, build and backtest your own model in the Model Builder, or review projected run totals on the MLB Run Prediction Model page.
