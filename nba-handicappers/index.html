In the NBA, timing is the skill

The NBA rewards information speed more than any other sport. A star downgraded at shootaround, a quiet rest day, a back-to-back spot, and the total swings before most people have read the news. A tout can claim they "had the under" all season and only count the nights the news broke their way. A timestamped record removes that wiggle room entirely, so the cappers near the top of this board are the ones who consistently locked a number before the move, which is exactly what their average odds reveal.

How to read an NBA capper's record

Start with units and ROI over a meaningful sample size, then look at the market split on their profile. NBA is the deepest player-prop sport going, and a capper who is genuinely good at points, rebounds and assists props is a very different animal from a spread bettor. The profile shows where the profit actually came from, so you are not crediting a prop specialist for a spread record they never had.

Red flags in NBA touting

Be most suspicious in the sport where records are easiest to reset. Watch for a "record" that quietly restarts after a cold week, prop wins posted with no losing slips beside them, and unit claims with no timestamps in a market that moves by the minute. On this board none of that is possible: losing picks stay graded, every entry is dated, and the math is identical for every account, so a hot stretch cannot be laundered into a season-long reputation.
