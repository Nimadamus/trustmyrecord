The third result that changes everything: the draw

Every other sport on this site has two outcomes. Soccer has three, and that single fact reshapes how the markets work and how records have to be judged. A draw is not a glitch or a push, it is a live, bettable result that lands often, which is exactly why a coin-flipper can look hot for a week and why the draw must be graded as its own outcome. The tracker treats it as a first-class result: it settles the match result on the 90-minute scoreline including stoppage time, so a draw grades as a draw, and a draw-no-bet refund grades as a push, never reinterpreted after the whistle.

Soccer bet types

Match result (1X2), double chance and draw no bet

The core three-way market and its safety variants: 1X2 backs a single outcome, double chance covers two of the three, and draw no bet refunds on the draw. The tracker grades each against the official result.

Asian and European handicaps

Goal-line handicaps that turn a lopsided match into a fairer price, from whole-goal European lines to split Asian lines like -0.75. Both settle on the adjusted margin.

Goals markets: over/under, team totals and BTTS

Totals such as over/under 2.5 goals, single-team totals, and both teams to score, the markets that let you bet the shape of a game without picking a winner.

Player and game props

Anytime and first goalscorer, shots and shots on target, player cards and team bookings, and corner totals where your book lists them, each tied to the player or number you locked.

Why the draw and the global calendar hide records

No sport is easier to cherry-pick. A tipster posts a Saturday Premier League winner, goes silent through three midweek losers, then resurfaces for the weekend, and with a three-way market even guesswork hits enough draws to fill a highlight reel. Add the sheer volume of competitions and a raw "we went 9-2 last week" becomes impossible to audit. A locked, timestamped record closes the loophole: the losing 1X2 tickets and the missed BTTS bets stay on the page beside the winners, so sample size across the leagues you can actually see beats anyone's single cup-final screenshot.

Frequently asked questions

How does the tracker grade a soccer draw?
The 1X2 result is settled on the 90-minute scoreline including stoppage time, so a draw grades as a draw on the moneyline rather than being pushed or ignored. Double chance, draw no bet and BTTS are graded against that same official result.
Can I track picks across different leagues and tournaments?
Yes. Premier League, La Liga, Champions League, Europa League, MLS, Liga MX, World Cup and Euro qualifiers, domestic cups and international friendlies all roll into one verified soccer record, and your profile shows a competition-by-competition breakdown.
Which soccer markets can I log?
Match result (1X2) including the draw, double chance, draw no bet, Asian and European handicaps, totals such as over/under 2.5 goals, team totals, and both teams to score where supported. Each locks before kickoff and auto-grades on the final result.
Why do you track units and ROI instead of just win percentage?
A long string of short-priced favorites and double-chance bets can win most of the time and still lose money. Units and ROI measure actual profitability over a real sample size.
